# Niche, Offer, and Go-To-Market Focus

## Selected Starting Niche

Start with appointment booking for local service businesses that already collect inbound or semi-warm leads but respond slowly or inconsistently.

Recommended first vertical: home-service providers with high job value, such as solar installers, roofing companies, HVAC companies, or renovation contractors.

Why this niche:

- Leads often arrive from forms, ads, marketplaces, referrals, or old quote requests, which makes consent and source tracking more realistic than scraped cold lists.
- Speed-to-lead matters. A call within minutes can materially improve booking rates.
- Average deal values are high enough to justify paid pilots and success fees.
- The sales motion is simple enough for an AI agent: confirm interest, qualify fit, answer basic objections, and book a human consultation.
- The buyer is usually owner-led or sales-manager-led, so pilots can close without enterprise procurement.

## Initial Customer Profile

Target clients:

- 5-50 person local service businesses.
- Spending at least $2,000/month on ads or lead marketplaces.
- Receiving at least 100 leads/month.
- Missing calls, delaying follow-up, or relying on overloaded sales reps.
- Willing to provide consent-backed lead lists and a human closer for qualified opportunities.

Avoid for the first 90 days:

- Debt collection, healthcare treatment advice, financial products, political campaigns, insurance policy sales, and any category where a bad call creates severe legal or brand risk.
- Businesses that want to upload scraped phone lists.
- Clients that cannot define a clear call outcome.

## Core Offer

Position the product as a managed AI lead-response and appointment-booking service.

Primary promise:

> We call your warm leads quickly, qualify them, handle common objections, and book appointments for your human sales team.

The AI agent should not claim to close complex sales. The MVP goal is to create more qualified human conversations at a lower cost than hiring another SDR.

## First Campaign Outcome

Primary measurable outcome: booked sales consultation.

Secondary outcomes:

- Qualified lead requiring human follow-up.
- Callback requested.
- Not interested.
- Bad number.
- Voicemail reached.
- Do-not-call requested.

Success threshold for the pilot:

- At least 8-12% of connected conversations produce a booked consultation or qualified callback.
- Cost per booked consultation stays below the client's current paid-ad cost per booked appointment.
- Opt-out and complaint rates remain low enough to keep campaign risk acceptable.

## Lead Types to Support First

Acceptable pilot lead sources:

- Inbound web forms where the customer consented to be contacted.
- Recent ad leads from Google, Meta, TikTok, LinkedIn, or lead marketplaces.
- Event/webinar/demo signups.
- Existing customer reactivation lists with documented business relationship.
- Abandoned quote or estimate requests.

Lead fields required before dialing:

- Full name.
- Phone number.
- Lead source.
- Consent timestamp or lawful contact basis.
- Timezone or postal code.
- Product/service interest.
- Campaign/client identifier.
- Optional notes from form submission.

## Sample Client-Facing Packages

Pilot package:

- $750 setup fee.
- $500/month includes 2,000 connected minutes.
- $0.18 per connected minute overage.
- Optional $25 per booked consultation.

Growth package:

- $1,500 setup fee.
- $1,500/month includes 7,500 connected minutes.
- $0.14 per connected minute overage.
- Optional success fee negotiated by industry value.

Done-for-you campaign package:

- $2,000 setup fee.
- $2,500/month includes call script design, campaign operations, weekly optimization, and reporting.
- Usage billed separately or bundled with a margin target above 65%.

## Positioning Against Alternatives

Compared with a human SDR:

- Lower marginal cost per call.
- Faster response to inbound leads.
- Consistent script adherence.
- Scales during peak lead periods.

Compared with SMS/email automation:

- Better for high-intent leads that need a conversation.
- Captures objections and buying intent.
- Can transfer or book appointments immediately.

Compared with generic AI calling tools:

- Built around consent-aware targeted marketing.
- Client campaigns are reviewed before launch.
- Sarvam creates a future path to multilingual campaigns.

## Sales Motion for First Customers

Discovery questions:

- How many leads do you receive each month?
- What percentage are called within five minutes?
- How many leads never get reached?
- What is a booked appointment worth?
- What is your current cost per booked appointment?
- Do your lead forms include consent to call?
- What common objections do prospects raise?

Pilot close:

- Ask for one narrow list, one offer, one outcome, and 30 days.
- Start with 300-1,000 leads or 1,000-2,000 connected minutes.
- Require approval of script, caller identity, consent source, and opt-out process before launch.

## Decision

Build the first version for warm-lead appointment booking in high-value local services. The service should sell more qualified appointments, not call volume.
